Definition of Personal Information

"Personal Information" refers to all kinds of information related to an identified or identifiable natural person, recorded electronically or by other means, excluding information that has been anonymized.

"Sensitive Personal Information" refers to Personal Information that, once leaked or illegally used, may easily lead to the infringement of a natural person's human dignity or endanger his or her personal and property safety, including biometrics, religious beliefs, specific identities, medical and health information, financial accounts, whereabouts and other information, as well as Personal Information of minors under 14 years old.

“Processing” of Personal Information includes the collection, storage, use, processing, transmission, provision, disclosure and deletion, etc. of personal information.

About the use of cookies 

Cookies may be used by us to provide you with customized information from our web site. A cookie is an element of data that a web site can send to your browser, which may then store it on your system. Cookies allow us to understand who has seen which pages and advertisements, to determine how frequently particular pages are visited and to determine the most popular areas of our web site. Cookies may also allow us to make our web site more user friendly by, for example, allowing us to save your password so that you do not have to re-enter it every time to visit our web site.

We use cookies so that we can give you a better experience when you return to our web site. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies. You do not have to accept cookies, and you should read the information that came with your browser software to see how you can set up your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, this will give you the opportunity to decide whether to accept it. If you disable cookies from your browser you may not be able to access certain features of a particular web site.

Safeguards for Personal Information

DSA protects your Personal Information in the following main aspects:

  • Collection, use and provision of Personal Information: DSA complies with the principles of lawfulness, legitimacy, necessity and good faith in processing of your Personal Information.
  • Formulate and publicize Personal Information processing rules: DSA complies with the principles of openness and transparency in making public the rules for processing Personal Information and expressly indicate the purpose, method and scope of such handling.
  • Implementation of security systems and measures: In processing Personal Information, DSA always abides by applicable Personal Information protection laws and regulations. To ensure the security of Personal Information processing, DSA will take information security measures in compliance with relevant standards, manage Personal Information, restrict the means of external dissemination of Personal Information and prevent unauthorized access according to relevant systems, and prevent Personal Information leakage to the utmost extent.
  • Respecting the rights of information subjects: When you request the disclosure, correction or deletion of your Personal Information, or refuse the use or provision of your Personal Information, DSA will respect the rights of the information subject and respond and handle the relevant request in a timely manner in accordance with the requirements of the corresponding laws and regulations.
  • Development and continuous improvement of Personal Information management system
  • DSA 's management and employees understand the importance of Personal Information protection. DSA will develop, fully implement and continuously improve relevant Personal Information management systems to ensure the reasonable use and protection of Personal Information.
  • DSA is constantly optimizing its organizational and technical measures for Personal Information protection. DSA will strictly limit the access to and use of Personal Information through a sound internal Personal Information management system and will also take necessary security technical measures to protect your Personal Information from unauthorized access, destruction, modification or disclosure.
  • The Internet environment is not completely secure. DSA will do its best to ensure and guarantee the security of any information you send us. In the event of an incident endangering network security, DSA will promptly address the security risks, take appropriate remedial measures, and report to the relevant competent authorities in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. At the same time, DSA will, in accordance with the requirements of laws and regulations, promptly inform you of the basic situation and possible impact of the security incident, the measures DSA has taken or will take, and DSA’ remedial measures for you. DSA will inform you of the relevant information of the incident in a timely manner by SMS notification, e-mail, telephone and other contact information reserved by you. If it is difficult to inform you on an individual basis, DSA will make an announcement in a reasonable and effective way, such as issuing a notice on DSA’s official website.
